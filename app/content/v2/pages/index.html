Young Citizens' Council for the Ocean

A project by Nina Dietz & Jan Feuring Founders of Blue Citizen e.V.

We all live on the Blue Planet. Being a Blue Citizen is our identity and our shared responsibility — sustained by the tides connecting us across the globe.

The Young Citizens' Council for the Ocean is a youth-led initiative that reimagines what citizenship means in an ocean-connected world. While the project began in a European context, its horizon is cosmopolitan: there is only one ocean, and it connects us all.

Through knowledge (to understand), culture (to feel), and governance (to act), we developed the Blue Citizen Compass — a framework that links values, education, and policy proposals to strengthen meaningful participation in ocean governance.

But what is a Blue Citizen?

A Blue Citizen recognizes that citizenship is more than belonging — it is responsibility for our shared ocean and the right to actively shape the political decisions that affect it.

Explore our currents to discover:

  • the definition young people developed,
  • the principles and policy recommendations of the Compass,
  • educational resources on Blue Citizenship,
  • and ways to take part.

The Council highlights the visionary and innovative perspectives of young people — not because they are “the leaders of tomorrow,” but because they are already political actors today. Their imagination and sense of intergenerational justice help open new pathways toward sustainable ocean futures.

Yet Blue Citizenship is not limited to one generation or one region. It is an open invitation: anyone who cares for the ocean and believes in shared democratic responsibility can be a Blue Citizen.

There is only one ocean.
